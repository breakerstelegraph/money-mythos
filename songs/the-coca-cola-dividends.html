Song

The Coca-Cola
Dividends

On Patience · On the Long View

In 1919, the Great War is over. Coca-Cola just went public at $40 a share. Then the sugar market collapses. Stock crashes to $19. Wall Street calls it a disaster. But in a tiny farm town — a back-road market man sees something else. He sees a nickel habit that won't quit.
This is a nickel habit that won't let go.
Verse
We go to work, we got no big break — the sun burns through another day. Lord knows the heavy load, the banks go round. In this heat, in this slow grind, there's one small way round.
The nickel they won't let go — it's a steel-cold certainty. A spell they've got. When every cell says no more, they'll steal just one cold shot.
The little stream of paper grows. Doors collect, windows close. But they can't quit the habit — so it lives out there on the road.
Chorus
Buy the stock, oldest dream — turn that bottle to a long-term thing. When the ground runs dry, when the work runs thin, the dividends will flow. And when you buy the stock you buy the minutes where your troubles don't show.
Verse
You got nothing, but that ain't no way. You got a nickel for a Coke, don't you?
I've been at this window with leather and pain, watching every broke man walk out holding something.
Dollars on the day — but you can't ignore: when they've got to have it, all that thirst, we want more. Sugar market crash? Yet the ticker's looking real. The price got cut in half — but the habit is the deal.
Long term. Good night again. Long term. Good night with big gains.
Bridge
We didn't strike oil. We didn't mine gold. We bought an old story that never got old. Plain-based, iron-sure, and wouldn't change. Let the years whisper: millionaires.
Next in line — what do you need? Farm it home on a little seed. Moving on — one share more. Time's right here, we keep the score. One share's enough to start the spin — let the years do what they know.
Outro
We didn't strike oil, we didn't mine gold. We bought an old story, and never got old. Plain-based, iron-sure, and wouldn't change. Let the years whisper millionaires.
Trust the habit. From the nickel you can't let go.
Hey, yo.
